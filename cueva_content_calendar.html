The Rules That Don't Change
01
Posts Build. Stories Maintain.
Posts are permanent. Stories disappear. Use stories for time-sensitive moments and casual connection. Posts are for the work that should last. One post per three to five stories roughly.
02
Post and Close the App.
Put it up. Walk away. Don't check it for at least two hours. The work is done when you hit post, not when the comments come in. Remove the dopamine loop from the act of creating.
03
One Brand at a Time.
When you sit down to post, you are one brand. Not three. Cave voice. Cueva Design voice. John voice. They do not mix. The calendar tells you which one today is for.
04
Content Already Exists.
You almost always have something to post. Check your camera roll before you decide you have nothing. Archive trips, old rolls, process shots from orders, rebrand assets. The content is there.
05
Comments Are Optional. Hearts Are Not.
Heart every comment. Reply with a single 🙏🏽 to the strongest one if you feel like it. Consistency of posting matters more than comment volume.
06
Photos Don't Expire.
There is no timeline shame. A photo from two years ago posted today is still just a photo. Post it when it feels right. The archive is a resource, not a source of guilt.
Monthly Creative Focus
Current Month — June 2026
Set at the start of each month. One deep study. One personal piece intention.
Deep Study Subject
Japanese Designers and Material as Philosophy
Issey Miyake, Yohji Yamamoto, Junya Watanabe, Jun Takahashi. Go into their influences, writing, and process. Not just the aesthetic. How do they think about fabric, construction, deconstruction, imperfection.
Non-Client Piece Intention
The Unfinished Painting
Background exists. Before touching it again ask: What do I want to reveal this month. Not what to paint. What to reveal. The answer becomes the painting.
The Question Before Every Design Session
What is this trying to reveal?
Answer this in your sketchbook before opening Illustrator. Client work or personal. Every time.
Monthly Focus Framework — How to Set It
Deep Study
One designer, movement, symbol, tradition, or material. Go all the way in, not broad. Their influences, their writing, their process, not just their output. Go deep enough that you have something to say about it that nobody else is saying.
Personal Piece
Ask this question first before anything else is decided: What do I want to reveal this month, and what is the right form for it. The form follows the revelation, not the other way around.
Specificity Is the Whole Game
Consuming broadly builds vocabulary. Going deep on one thing builds voice. You need both but the deep study is the counterweight. One thing per month, all the way in.
